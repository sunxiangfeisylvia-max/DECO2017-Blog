It feels a little strange that I am finally starting this assignment. For the past few weeks, most things still felt like setup, getting used to the course, understanding the tools, and trying not to get lost every time coding comes up. I would not say I am very confident with coding yet. I can follow examples and understand basic ideas, but when I think about building a full web app by myself, it still feels a bit intimidating. Because of that, starting this project makes the whole semester feel more real. It is the point where I need to stop just reading and actually begin thinking about what I can make.

My first feeling about the project is that it is more open than I expected, but also more demanding. There is a lot of freedom in choosing the kind of community hub to design, which is exciting, but that also means I need to make good decisions early. If the idea is too broad, it will probably become hard to manage. If it is too simple, it may not feel like a real community platform. So even though I have not made any final decisions yet, I already feel that choosing the right direction will matter a lot.

What stands out to me most right now is that the project is not really asking for a random social site. It is asking for something built around a specific community, and I think that changes everything. A good community hub needs a reason to exist. People need a shared interest, shared needs, or shared goals. Otherwise it just becomes a website with pages and features but no real purpose. That is probably the main thing I got from reading through the task. Before thinking about layout or interface, I need to think about what kind of group would actually benefit from having its own online space.

Another thing I noticed is that the project is not only about ideas. There are also technical limits and requirements that I have to keep in mind from the beginning. The site needs to be responsive, accessible, and built with the required tools. I think this is the part that makes me a little nervous, because I am still not fully comfortable with coding, especially when different parts of a system need to work together. At the same time, I think these limits are useful, because they stop me from imagining something too unrealistic.

Right now, I think my biggest task is not design in the visual sense. It is deciding on a community that is focused, interesting, and possible to build. I want something that has a clear purpose and a few strong features, instead of too many weak ones. At this stage, I am still at the beginning, but I think that is a good place to be. I am not trying to solve everything yet. I am just trying to understand the project properly and make smarter choices before I start building.
