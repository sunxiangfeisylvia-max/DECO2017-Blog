I have not started working on the coding yet, but I definitely think that I am doing some crucial work now in my own particular way. The main thing that I was focused on for most of the week is determining what type of community hub I would like to develop. In the beginning, I thought that this was going to be the simplest task since, basically, I just needed to select an option that sounded good to me and get down to work. As you can see, this is not how things actually went. Rather, I was switching between concepts, which helped me understand that it is actually crucial to choose a topic at all.

One of the first concepts that came to mind was the creation of a generic students community. It initially seemed like a good choice since it would have been relatively easy to justify. Students share needs, and there were a lot of potential topics that could be covered, ranging from studying strategies to campus information, events, and resources. However, soon enough it became clear that such a concept might get out of hand. An all-purpose student platform could have become anything, which seems convenient but is very complex indeed. With a broader audience, it becomes increasingly hard to define the primary goal for a certain platform.

This led me to consider designing something for new students specifically. This idea is much more powerful since the target audience becomes quite clear and their needs are better defined. New students require information that is both useful and practical, including food options, study locations, campus areas that can be used to good effect, and even simple things that might prove confusing upon arriving on campus. I believe this idea is one of the more feasible approaches because it is fairly easy to explain the need for this type of information-sharing. On the other hand, I have yet to convince myself that this idea will satisfy all requirements of the brief and be sufficiently engaging as a community.

I was also thinking of a movie related community, preferably one centered on independent/art house movies. On my part, I believe that the latter is a better choice since it carries a sense of identity and specificity. It will be easier for me to envision users coming back to discuss movies, rate them, or simply respond to posts. However, I have yet to decide if the subject is practical enough for my prototype since it is clearly community-oriented but difficult to define in terms of core functionality. I would rather not select a theme based solely on personal interest unless I am certain that it can be made functional through a web application.

This is where I am right now, but the process itself has proven to be invaluable. What I now realize is that while I am certainly selecting an interesting topic, I am also setting the boundaries for complexity, needs of the users, and scope of the project. At this stage, I am attempting to strike a balance between being sufficiently specific while remaining manageable enough to develop. I have not finalized my decision yet, but I am getting closer by eliminating topics that are overly broad, too flimsy, or simply too grandiose.
