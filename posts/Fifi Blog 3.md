Now, however, even though I have not yet begun to code, I have already progressed further than before, and the project seems less conceptual than before. While initially, much of my thought process was oriented towards selecting a proper community and avoiding broadening the concept beyond recognition, now I am beginning to transition from selecting the topic to designing its architecture. Rather than focusing on determining the nature of the platform as an abstract concept, I now focus on what it should offer to a user. This is allowing me to think more in concrete terms.

The thing that was most helpful for me to do so far is creating some low fidelity wireframes. They were not very advanced yet, but they gave me an insight into what the platform might actually require. I started off from considering the most basic scenario of a journey through the platform by the registered user – entering the hub, looking for something, and what one can do there after.

Currently, my most revisited pages are a homepage/primary feed page, a post detail page, a page for posting something, and some sort of saved/personal content page. The homepage must be key since it will establish the overall feel of the entire community space. It should indicate the type of content that is valued, the organization of posts, and the actions available. The post detail page will facilitate the actual interaction on the site, through the reading and responding to the content. I believe a page for creating a post is mandatory, as the primary purpose of the platform involves sharing information. I am also contemplating the inclusion of a user/profile page or post-saving option within the initial version, as it could be an overly broad scope.

Creating wireframes also meant that I needed to give more thought to information architecture. On paper, it is simple to claim that a community hub should encourage helpful exchanges, but when I started working on my wireframe designs, I needed to consider which aspects should take precedence in the initial stages of the process. For instance, would the home page prioritize categories, new content, or featured content? Would it encourage the users to explore or create content first? This choice will influence not just the design, but the user experience as well. Right now, it seems better to emphasize exploring and discovering, as newcomers must know about the community before participating in it.

A final aspect which became apparent due to the creation of the wireframe was the necessity versus optionality of certain features within the application. While initially, there were numerous potential ideas for the development of the app, the process of actually designing an interface has made me become more pragmatic. Certain things, such as direct messaging or more sophisticated matching methods, no longer appear necessary for the first prototype of the app.

I believe this is the most productive stage till now since it bridges the gap between conception and implementation. Though I have not yet begun coding, my project is moving from just being an idea floating around in my mind to becoming a framework consisting of pages, priorities, and user actions. The next thing on the agenda would be improving these wireframes and using them to determine the minimum requirements for the software platform.
